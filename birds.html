Comorant

Cormorants have relatively short wings due to their need to swim. Because of this, they expend more energy in flight than any other flying bird Cormorants share many features with shags. Depending on who you ask, there might not be any relevant distinction between them.

Hairy woodpecker

Hairy Woodpeckers are common in mature woodlands with medium to large trees. They also occur in woodlots, suburbs, parks, and cemeteries. You can find them equally commonly in coniferous forests, deciduous forests, or mixtures, and generally up to about 6,500 feet elevation.

Emu

Emus are soft-feathered, brown, flightless birds with long necks and legs, and can reach up to 1.9 metres (6.2 ft) in height. Emus can travel great distances, and when necessary can sprint at 50 km/h (31 mph); they forage for a variety of plants and insects, but have been known to go for weeks without eating.
